Overview

  • The world map is from Divine Right. Each hex is 50 miles. The campaign starts in Muetar (yellow), in the city of Basimar.
  • Drop fractions unless stated otherwise.
  • The silver coin (s.c.) is the standard currency. A gold coin is a big deal to the average person. Imagine a silver coin is USD $10 and a gold coin is USD $100, roughly.
  • To-hit bonuses are +1 per level for fighters, +1/2 per level for Magic-Users, and +2/3rds per level for everyone else.
  • Many of the classes are a little bit different. See the class pages below.
  • You can get class feats at levels 1, 4, 8, and 12. These are considered exceptional; not every 1st level fighter will have a feat.
  • Saving throws are Target 20: roll D20 plus modifiers and try to get 20+. The standard saves at level 0 are:
    Doom +5, Slice +4, Bind +3, Blast +2, and Hex +1.
    These all go up by +1 every level/HD, including first.
  • Hit points are rerolled every level, keeping higher total (or lower, if being level-drained).
  • At 0 hit points you fall prone and must save vs. Doom or die. If you save, you are still incapacitated, on the ground, and will bleed out eventually. If tended to, you can get back to 1hp and mobility. In any case you are injured (half speed, half actions) until back at max HP.
  • With a full night's rest, double your current HP, but regain only one spell slot.
  • Weapons are different and have special rules based on size and type. See Weaponry below.
  • Critical hits trigger on maximum damage rolls, and explode. Blunt weapons do not crit. Weapons 2+ sizes smaller than the target do not crit, unless they are piercing weapons. So a (VS) punch cannot crit a (M) human, but a (VS) knife can. Only things that would normally roll to hit crit on damage.
  • Ranged attacks are -1 to-hit for every full 30' of range. Indoors most shots are direct fire. Outdoors, more arc can be used and maximum range is tripled. Shots generally are off-target by 1' per pip. Aiming at a specific spot in the ground is AC 10 (it's small, but it's not moving).
  • Lamp oil does not burn well enough to be a weapon. Alchemists sell alchemists fire for 25 s.c.
  • Equipment is quite different; see below.
  • Item-based encumbrance. Each 'enc' is about 5 lbs.
  • Upkeep cost is (Level^2 x 100) s.c. per month. This covers living expenses, taxes, money-handling fees, repairs and mending, storage, stabling fees, clothing, etc.

Adeese is the epicenter of many sects, though the ascendent sect in Muetar is of St. Leoir based in Beolon.

Turning Undead: Each round, resolved before initiative, your Cleric may make an attempt to turn one type of undead by brandishing their holy symbol and uttering the Law against such abominations. Each type may only be attempted to be turned once per combat. The chance of a successful turn is 3-in-6 adjusted by the difference between the caster's level and the target's HD. If the turn is successful, 2D6 HD worth of creatures (minimum 1 creature) are affected. They will attempt to flee, or, if the turn roll succeeded by 3+, they are destroyed immediately and utterly.

Spells: Clerics prepare a spell into a spell slot by praying for 10 minutes. A night's rest restores 1 spell


Cleric Feats

  • Authority. You can turn/destroy elementals as well as undead.
  • Dispensation. You have no weapons restrictions.
  • Survivor of Trials. You are take half damage from one element (fire, cold, etc.)
  • Heretical Epiphany. Replace half your cleric spell list with magic-user spells, rolled randomly.
  • Lightbringer. You turn undead as if your level was 2 higher.
  • New Faith. You can't wear armor and can only wield a staff, and you must not kill, but you know and cast spells as if you were two levels higher.
  • Abjurer. You can cast dispel magic once/day.
  • Researcher. If there is a language you could possibly know, you can identify it, and have a 50% chance of having learned it.

Dwarf

Dwarves are created from stone-made-flesh by other dwarfs, and the cost of this process is handed onto dwarves as a debt. Dwarves call their lands Ghem, and the true stone that allows the creation of new dwarves only exists in Rosengg, Alazk, and Aws Noir, thus while dwarves are seen in many places, their society is still centered around Ghem (and even Aws Noir dwarves are not considered 'true dwarves' by some.)

Life Debt: Dwarves start play owing (1D6 x 1D6) x 1,000 s.c of debt to their Maker. Stonesense: Dwarves have 2-in-6 chance to detect construction tricks or larger room traps when searching.

Infravision: Dwarves have 60' infravision.

Keen Ears: Dwarves have a 2-in-6 listening chance undeground.


Dwarf Feats

  • Upswing. When an enemy attacks you and rolls a natural 1 you may use your reaction to make an attack against them.
  • Frenzy. Attack all adjacent foes as a single multi-target attack.
  • The Eye. Architecture sense is 5-in-6.
  • Power Swing. Once per combat, instead of rolling weapon damage, do maximum damage (and crit).
  • Outer Stone. Ignore damage dice that roll 1. If all dice are negated ignore the bonuses too. Increases to 1-2 at level 5, and 1-3 at level 9.
  • Rage Drunk. When intoxicated your attacks are -1D6 to-hit but do that much additional damage.
  • Inheritance. Inherit a +2 battleaxe, or other item the DM invents.
  • Crystallized Heart. After a night's rest gain temporary hit points equal to your level until your next night's rest.
  • Inner Stone. You gain 1 DR against all damage and don't need to breathe.
  • The Nose: Dwarf can detect nearby precious metals by smell, can taste richness of veins.

Elf

Elves come from the kingdom of Neuth, in the great forest of the northwest. They have a long hatred of the goblins of Zorn. Their society is currently dominated by a faction called the Returners, who came back from a fallen colony to the west.

Spells: Elves don't memorize spells like MUs, but instead can hold (Level + 1 + Int mod) spells in their mind "readied", and cast them using spell slots. Readying a different spell (from a book or scroll) takes 10 minutes.

Keen Senses: Elves have a 2-in-6 chance of hearing noise, or detect hidden or secret doors.

Infravision: Elves have 60' infravision.

Elder Remnant: Elves are immune to the paralysis effect of ghouls.


Elf Feats

  • Agent Training. Gain thief skills as a thief of your level/2.
  • Heartspell. Pick a Readied spell. It now always freely Ready for you.
  • Backswing. Once/round you may reroll a to-hit roll if it is a natural 1-10.
  • Singing Bow. You may fire up to level/2 arrows in a round, all at separate targets.
  • Spell Arrow. When you cast a spell you may fire an arrow, and the spell targets where the arrow hits. Doesn't work with self-targeting spells.
  • Quick Reaction. You may always act in a surprise round.
  • Dead eyes. Once per combat, if you spend a round focussed on a target, the next round you will have +10 to-hit the target.
  • Lore Trained. Have a 50% chance of identifying any magic item with brief inspection.
  • Forbidden Songs. Can ready Cleric spells, may pick one for each spell level.

Sweep: Fighters may attack all adjacent foes with the given hit dice. (Roughly level/4.) Apply the same single hit die and damage roll to all targets.


Fighter Feats

  • Battle Trance. Once per combat you may try to save vs. Doom as a reaction. If you succeed you gain +4 to-hit, +4 on saves, and 20 temporary hit points until the end of combat.
  • Martial Training. Your unarmed attacks count as weapons, can critical, and do D4 damage, increasing in die size levels 3, 5, 7, and 9, and count as magic weapons.
  • Blessed. +4 on all saves.
  • Rapid Strike. If you made up to 1 attack last round, you may make 2 attacks this round.
  • Ranger. You can survive and track in the wilderness. You have an animal companion with a strong and non-magical bond, with HD equal to your level/2.
  • Famous. You are known in song in a kingdom you choose. You may reroll reaction rolls with locals if you bring it up.
  • Sweep Attack. You may make multiple attacks on creatures whose total HD is no higher than your level.
  • Expert Rider. Any mount you ride has +2 to-hit and saves, and +50% hit points. You will not get knocked off your horse involuntarily.

Small: Halflings are -2 to be hit by Large (and larger) sized creatures. Halfing armor costs and weighs half as much.

Thief Skills: Halflings can allocate points to Thief skills. Unlike theives they do not have to put 1 rank into every skill at first level. Medium armor and above will preclude Move Silently skills.

Swampfolk: Halflings get +2 to their stealth skills in swampy or grassy areas.


Halfling Feats

  • Sturdy. When you would take fatal damage, you may take half instead. (This still might kill you.)
  • Eyes Closed. Whenever you roll a 1 on a 1D20, you may reroll it but must take the result.
  • Boat Folk. You are an expert swimmer, diver, pilot, and fisherfolk.
  • Yando Ties. You can get help and support and one follower from the Yando.
  • Trick Shot. If you miss with a sling shot you may roll to-hit on a target adjacent to the original target, once per attack.
  • Sales Knack. You may reroll a reaction check, once per day.
  • Family Recipe. You maintain a pouch of special ingredients. Given 10 minutes you can whip them into a poultice that cures 50% of lost HP and allows new saves for poison and disease. It takes a week to replenish the pouch.
  • The Gift. You can understand and make yourself understood to animals.
  • Witch-Touched. You can ready and cast spells as an Elf half your level (round up).

Spellcasting: MUs keep spellbooks and can have more spells than they memorize. MUs can memorize from spellbooks or scrolls (there is no difference between the two). Copying a scroll is difficult, requiring a day, (100 x Spell level) sc of materials, and a successful save vs. Hex. If the save is failed the effort is fruitless; if it is failed with a natural 1 the original spell is lost.


Magic-User Feats

Foci must be crafted personally by an MU and cost a week of work, plus 100 sc of special material, x10 for each additional feat's ability supported. You can only have one focus.
  • Familiar. You gain a familiar, a loyal and helpful 1HD creature. With a round of concentration you can sense through its senses instead of yours and communicate with it telepathically. If it dies you can attract another after a week.
  • Split Mind. You are ambidextrous, and you can cast two spells at once if both your hands are free.
  • Orb Caster. You need a focus to cast spells, but you can double the range or duration of spells you cast when you cast them.
  • Wand Caster. You need a focus to cast spells, but you can memorize +1 spell per level.
  • Gem Caster. You need a focus to cast spells, but you can store spells in your focus whose total levels are no greater than your level.
  • Apt Pupil. You make magic items at half cost and time, and never fail to copy spells successfully.
  • Angelic Lore. Per spell level you can cast, one random Cleric spell of that level is learnable as if it was an MU spell.
  • Thaumaturge. You can wield any weapon and wear any non-metallic armor.
  • Spillover. You can express cantrip-like manifestations of your currently memorized spells. They are pretty weak.
  • Blood Caster. After casting a spell you can maintain memory of it, but take 1D4 psychic damage per level of the spell.

Thief Skills: Thief skills are chance-in-6. At first level, thieves may distribute 14 points among them (but must put at least 1 point in each). For ranks above 5, you roll an extra die per rank above five, and only fail if they all come up 6.

The eight skills are: Climb, Find/Remove Traps, Hear Noise, Hide in Shadows, Move Silently, Open Locks, Pick Pockets, and Use Device.

Use Device: Allows the thief to activate and cast scrolls if the roll ismade.

Backstab: When attacking a unaware opponent from behind, the thief is +4 to-hit and double damage. This can be a ranged attack.


Thief Feats

  • Hidden Talent. You can learn and cast spells as a MU of half your level.
  • Bloodthirsty. You have +1 to-hit and damage against humans and demi-humans.
  • Resources. Once per session you can claim any item in your equipment if it would fit in a small sack and costs < 50 sc.
  • Deft Hands. You may throw a light weapon as your reaction. You may catch a thrown weapon if you make a save vs. Slice.
  • Payback. When you take melee damage you can sacrifice your next action to make an immediate backstab attempt against the attacker.
  • Faceless. You are an almost perfect mimic of others voices and mannerisms and good at disguises.
  • Rombunic Training. You are immune to poisons personally, and can maintain Level/2 doses of poisons on you of various kinds (paralytics, deadly poisons, hypnotics). Larger targets require more doses, and they must be tailored to humanoids or to beasts. Doses spoil and can be recovered 1/week.
  • All Skill. Up to (Level/2) times per session you may reroll any D6 roll you rolled, including for surprise, initiative, etc.
  • Made. You're a member of The Guild. You can spot members and imposters, and you can reroll a reaction check with civilians by dropping a hint.

Cleric Spells


Magic-User Spells


Level One

Charm Person. Range 40', Duration indefinite. Requires eye contact. Target creature with humanoid-like mind becomes devoted friend of caster. Doing tasks that harm the target's (real) devoted friends prompts a save vs. Hex to negate.

Feather Fall. Range 40', Duration indefinite, Reaction. A number of mid-air creatures/objects up to the caster's level lose vertical momentum and float down at 20' per round. The spell ends when the target lands.

Firebeam. Range 40', Duration Concentration up to 1/round level. A whip-like fiery beam flows from caster's hand, lashing at a target chosen each round, doing 1d8 damage (save vs. Slice negates).

Instant Servant. Range touch, Duration 1 hour/level. A free-standing object sprouts arms and legs and will obey the caster's orders literally. It has stats as a 0-level human, but is not intelligent or observant.

Light. Range 40', Duration 1 hour/level. Part of target pointed at glows with a flame-like aura the size and illumination of a torch. If cast on a creature's nose or eye it blinds them, save vs. Hex negates.

Magic Missile. Range 120', Instant. A force arrow forms in the air and strikes a target the caster can see, unerringly, for 1d6+1 piercing damage. An additional arrow forms at caster levels 3, 5, 7, etc. Targets are chosen before damage rolls.

Prestidigitation. Cantrip, Range 80', Instant. The caster may do small tricks, e.g. gusts of wind, ventriloquism, whispers, slamming or opening of simple unlocked portals or windows.

Shield. Range self, Duration 1h, Reaction. An invisible disk flits around the caster, giving them +5 AC and specifically negating magic missile attacks.

Sleep. Range 80', Instant. Produces a brief glittering field 10' in radius. Creatures in the field of 4 HD or less must save vs. Hex or fall into a very deep sleep for 1 hour. A creature may be jostled awake but will lose a turn waking up.

Wizard Lock. Range 40', Duration 1 day/level. Door, portal, or chest is held shut. The caster can freely open and close but no other character can without bashing the object in or magic such as knock or dispel magic.


Level Two

See Aura. Range self, Duration 1 round/level. The caster can see auras around enchanted, invisbile, and 'evil' objects.

Invisibility. Range touch, Duration until sunrise. Target creature or object turns invisible. It can see itself hazily.

Knock. Range 40', 1 roundlevel. Any object the wizard gestures at will open, including locks, bars, wizard locks, buckles, chests, bottles, etc.

Levitate. Range touched, Duration 1 minute/level. Willing touched creature floats in the air, and control its movement up or down 10' per round or cancel the spell at will.

Locate Object. Range 100' per level, Duration 1 turn/level. Caster sense direction of well-known or clearly visualized object or feature. Can be general type in which case the closest one is pointed towards.

Mirror Image. Range self, Duration 1 minute/level. (1d4+1) images of the caster spring out from their location doing various credible actions. An attack or spell that targets the caster (and hits its AC 10) will be found to have targeted an image instead, dispelling it.

Phantasmal Force. Range 200'. Duration concentration or 1h. The caster creates an illusion of an object, creature, or force within sight, and can move it around. If it is struck (AC 10) it is dispelled. Concentration is required to move it.

Read Minds. Range 40', Duration 1 hour/level. After casting this the caster can, as an action, read surface-level thoughts of sentient nearby beings, even ones out of sight e.g. in the next room.

Web. Range 40', Duration 1 hour/level. It is up to level/2 10'x10'x10' cubes; each must be anchored on opposite sides. A stuck creature can break lose with a save vs Bind or by doing 5 or by hacking the webs around a creature for 5 total points of slashing damage (AC 10, or AC 15 for a creature trying to free itself).


Level Three

Name
